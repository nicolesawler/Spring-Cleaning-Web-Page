As dreary winter comes to an end and the sunlight starts to stream in through your windows again, one thing becomes abundantly clear: the house really needs a good clean. The walls are covered in marks, the bathroom tiles need a lot of love and that smell in the fridge you’ve been ignoring is starting to permeate the house. You know what you need to do, but hearing the words “spring cleaning” makes your skin crawl.

5 ways to make spring cleaning suck less

1. Get a fresh start.

1. Get a fresh start.

A good spring clean relies on good cleaning products, so it’s important you load up on (environmentally friendly!) cleaners that work on different surfaces. When it comes to the sponge itself, freshen it up by squeezing it out and microwaving it on high for a minute.

2. Wax on, wax off.

2. Wax on, wax off.

To get your stovetop looking tip-top, scrub off the baked-on grime, then apply a thin layer of car wax and buff it off with a towel. This simple trick will not only leave your stovetop looking shiny and new, but it will make wiping off those inevitable future spills a whole lot easier.

3. Pray for rain.

3. Pray for rain.

If you have kids or pets, you will no doubt be accustomed to dirty marks all over your windows. Giving them a good wipe is an obvious must during your spring clean, but to do the job right, it’s best to wait and wash them on a cloudy day. Sunshine will cause the glass to dry too quickly, which will result in frustratingly streaky windows.

4. Full steam ahead.

4. Full steam ahead.

Did some comfort food explode in your microwave over the winter? Save your elbow grease and instead steam the stains before you scrub them. Simply mix two tablespoons of white vinegar and a few drops of essential oil with a cup of water in a microwave safe bowl, and then zap the mixture for five minutes.

5. Do double duty.

5. Do double duty.

Cleaning your house is one thing, keeping it clean is another challenge altogether. Get a head start by doubling up on doormats. By keeping one outside and one inside the front door, you’ll trap more dirt before it’s trampled across your freshly cleaned carpets. Since dirty doormats defeat the purpose, clean them both regularly.
